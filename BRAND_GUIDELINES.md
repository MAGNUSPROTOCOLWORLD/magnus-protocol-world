## 2) Full Technical Brand Guideline (Detailed)

## 2.1 Brand Overview
MAGNUS PROTOCOL is a next-generation protocol brand focused on artificial intelligence, secure infrastructure, and future-ready digital systems.  
The identity is designed to communicate trust, precision, and strategic scale from first contact.

The visual system is intentionally minimal and institutional:
- High-contrast black and metallic gold
- Geometric construction with disciplined spacing
- Reduced visual noise for premium perception

Brand promise:
- If MAGNUS PROTOCOL is present, execution quality and governance discipline are expected.

## 2.2 Brand Positioning

### Positioning Statement
MAGNUS PROTOCOL is the infrastructure-grade technology brand for organizations that need intelligence systems that are secure, scalable, and accountable.

### Strategic Attributes
- Premium: high-caliber, boardroom-ready presence
- Institutional: credible in regulated and high-trust environments
- Global: neutral, adaptable, cross-region compatible
- Futuristic: advanced without speculative styling
- Authoritative: clear standards, clear architecture

### Competitive Differentiation
- Not a consumer app identity
- Not a gaming visual language
- Not trend-led cyber aesthetics
- Built for long-term trust and cross-channel durability

## 2.3 Mission, Vision, and Purpose

### Mission
Design and deploy trusted intelligence infrastructure that remains secure, interpretable, and scalable under real-world operational demands.

### Vision
Enable a global technology environment where advanced intelligence systems can be deployed with confidence, accountability, and interoperability.

### Purpose
Place technology in service of human and institutional progress through responsible architecture and disciplined execution.

## 2.4 Tone of Voice

### Voice Characteristics
- Precise: factual language, low ambiguity
- Strategic: long-horizon framing with execution detail
- Institutional: governance-aware and credibility-first
- Composed: strong without aggressive posturing

### Writing Standards
- Prefer measurable claims over broad claims
- Use short paragraphs with clear intent
- Avoid speculative superlatives
- Avoid slogans that cannot be operationally defended

### Preferred Vocabulary
- build, engineer, govern, deploy, secure, scale, integrate, validate

### Avoid
- revolutionary, magical, unstoppable, destiny, limitless power

## 2.5 Logo System

### 2.5.1 Primary Logo (Main Lockup)
Composition:
- Symbol: geometric monogram emblem
- Wordmark: MAGNUS PROTOCOL in uppercase

Default treatment:
- Symbol and wordmark in metallic gold range
- Dark background only
- High-contrast, low-noise environment

### 2.5.2 Secondary Logo (Light Background)
Composition:
- Same geometry and proportions
- Black or deep graphite mark and wordmark on white
- No gradient dependence in compliance contexts

### 2.5.3 Icon-Only Version
Use case:
- Avatars, compact UI, in-product identifiers, watermarking

Rule:
- Use symbol only
- Remove micro-details when size is constrained

### 2.5.4 Horizontal and Stacked Variations
- Horizontal lockup: preferred for headers, documents, signatures
- Stacked lockup: preferred for social cards, covers, and narrow layouts
- Icon-only: preferred below minimum wordmark size threshold

### 2.5.5 Monochrome Compliance Versions
Required set:
- 100% black
- 100% white
- Single-color vector suitable for legal filing, embossing, and stamping

## 2.6 Clear Space and Usage Rules

### Clear Space
Define `X` as the inner stroke width of the monogram.

- Minimum clear space all sides: `1.5X`
- Premium compositions (hero/cover): `2X`
- No text, lines, icons, or page edges may enter clear space

### Minimum Size
- Full lockup digital: 140 px width minimum
- Icon-only digital: 24 px minimum
- Full lockup print: 30 mm minimum
- Icon-only print: 8 mm minimum

### Placement Rules
- Prioritize top-left or center alignment in formal layouts
- Keep logo off highly textured zones
- Ensure WCAG-aware contrast in digital placement

### Prohibited Manipulations
- Do not stretch, skew, rotate, or warp
- Do not alter letter spacing manually
- Do not swap official palette with unapproved colors
- Do not add shadows/glows that reduce precision
- Do not place over busy imagery without a control layer

## 2.7 Color Palette with HEX and Usage Rules

### Core Palette
- Obsidian: `#050505`
- Carbon: `#0D0D0D`
- Graphite: `#171717`
- Metallic Gold: `#C6A86A`
- Deep Gold: `#9C7B43`
- Soft White: `#F2F2EE`
- Silver Neutral: `#A7A7A0`

### Accent Palette
- Signal Blue: `#008FFF` (status glow, edge emphasis, controlled highlights only)

### Usage Logic
- Dark surfaces define brand atmosphere
- Gold identifies authority and branded action points
- White and silver support readability and hierarchy
- Blue is not a base brand color; use as precision accent only

### Accessibility and Contrast
- Body text on dark surfaces should meet at least AA contrast
- Avoid gold-on-white for long-form text
- Reserve metallic treatments for display contexts, not dense UI copy

## 2.8 Typography System

### Primary Families
- Display: Space Grotesk (500, 600)
- Body/UI: Plus Jakarta Sans (400, 500, 600)
- Data/Code/Technical: JetBrains Mono (400, 500)

### Hierarchy
- H1: 56-72 px desktop, 40-48 px tablet, 32-36 px mobile
- H2: 40-48 px desktop, 32-36 px tablet, 28-32 px mobile
- H3: 28-32 px desktop, 24-28 px tablet, 20-24 px mobile
- Body L: 18-20 px
- Body M: 16-18 px
- Body S: 14-15 px
- Caption: 12-13 px

### Typographic Rules
- Uppercase for labels, nav, and lockup contexts only
- Moderate tracking in headlines for premium calm
- Keep line length in long-form content to 60-75 characters
- No novelty techno fonts in production

## 2.9 Visual Language (Style, Textures, Backgrounds)

### Style Direction
- Minimal, engineered, atmospheric
- Geometric precision with restrained highlights
- Dimensional but not ornamental

### Background System
- Primary: deep black gradients and low-noise surfaces
- Secondary: subtle radial light fields to guide focus
- Optional: refined grid overlays at low opacity for technical framing

### Textures
- Use micro-grain only when needed to prevent banding
- No rough grunge textures
- No neon-heavy cyber motifs

### Imagery Direction
- High-contrast infrastructure visuals
- Abstract systems, nodes, and controlled light geometry
- No cartoon, anime, or game-render aesthetic

## 2.10 UI Direction (Buttons, Cards, Layout)

### Buttons
- Primary: gold fill, dark text, high contrast
- Secondary: transparent with gold border
- Tertiary: text-only in white/silver
- Radius: medium (10-12 px), no extreme pill style

### Cards
- Surface: carbon/graphite
- Border: subtle gold or neutral stroke
- Shadow: soft depth, no dramatic lift
- Interaction: minimal hover translation and border emphasis

### Layout
- Mobile-first responsive grid
- 4/8/12 column scaling (mobile/tablet/desktop)
- Section rhythm based on 8 px spacing scale
- Generous negative space in premium sections

### Motion
- Subtle fade/slide and staggered reveal only
- Duration range: 180ms to 600ms
- Disable decorative motion in reduced-motion contexts

## 2.11 Iconography Style

### Construction
- Geometric line icons with controlled corners
- Consistent stroke thickness (1.5-2 px digital)
- Minimal internal detail

### Color
- Default: soft white/silver on dark surfaces
- Emphasis: gold for active/priority states
- Use blue sparingly for technical signal states

### Rule
- Icons support content hierarchy; they do not dominate it

## 2.12 Do and Don't Examples

### Do
- Use official lockups from approved asset kit
- Keep backgrounds controlled and high contrast
- Maintain exact spacing and proportions
- Use restrained highlights and clean composition
- Test logo legibility at target deployment sizes

### Don't
- Do not apply heavy glow, bevel stacks, or lens clutter
- Do not crowd the logo near text or edges
- Do not place gold logo on bright gold backgrounds
- Do not re-draw symbol lines for convenience
- Do not mix in unapproved futuristic effects

## 2.13 Digital Usage (Website, Social Media, App)

### Website
- Header: horizontal lockup or icon plus wordmark
- Hero: primary black/gold lockup with controlled contrast
- Footer: simplified lockup for compact legibility

### Social Media
- Avatar: icon-only version
- Cover/banner: stacked or horizontal lockup based on crop
- Post templates: preserve clear space and dark premium base

### Investor Deck
- Cover slide: primary lockup, centered or upper-left
- Section dividers: icon-only watermark at low opacity
- Data slides: typography-first, minimal decorative treatment

### Product/App
- Splash: icon-only centered on dark base
- App tile: approved rounded-square app icon version
- In-app branding: use icon in nav and trust signals

## 2.14 Print Usage (Documents, Business Cards)

### Documents
- Legal and contracts: monochrome or black version
- Corporate reports: black/gold for cover, neutral internals
- Use vector files only (SVG, PDF, EPS)

### Business Cards
- Front: icon-only or compact lockup, high contrast
- Back: contact information with strict typographic hierarchy
- Finishing options: matte black stock, optional gold foil for premium line

### Print Production Rules
- Minimum 300 DPI raster where vector is unavailable
- CMYK conversions must be proofed before press
- Avoid metallic simulation in low-quality print processes

## 2.15 Favicon and App Icon Rules

### Favicon
- Master source: simplified icon-only SVG
- Required exports: 16x16, 32x32, 48x48
- No wordmark in favicon

### App Icon
- Master canvas: 1024x1024
- Content safe area: 72% center zone
- Background: dark neutral
- Symbol: centered, clear, and uncluttered
- No small text, no micro-details, no stacked taglines

## 3) Asset Production Checklist

### Required Deliverables
- Logo master (primary, secondary, monochrome)
- Horizontal, stacked, and icon-only lockups
- Favicon package (ICO + PNG + SVG)
- App icon package (iOS and Android sizes)

- Vector: SVG, EPS, PDF
